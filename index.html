Overview

The BMW Group, based in Munich, Germany, is a global manufacturer of premium automobiles and motorcycles. It also provides premium financial and mobility services.

CDH architecture overview

For the past several years, the BMW Group has been at the forefront of the automotive industry’s digital transformation, using data and predictive analytics.

Empowering a Data-Driven Approach

In response to challenges, BMW Group re-architected its on-premises data lake to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) Cloud.

According to Kai Demtröder, BMW Group Vice President, "To stay innovative, we are focusing on creating new digital and connected experiences."

The migration to AWS allowed BMW Group to democratize data usage, making data easily accessible for internal teams and addressing emerging use cases.

Democratizing Data Usage at Scale

BMW Group uses AWS services like Amazon Athena, Amazon S3, AWS Glue, and more to process and combine anonymized data from vehicle sensors.

The Cloud Data Hub (CDH) facilitates data ingestion, transformation, and usage, allowing both data providers and consumers to operate autonomously and efficiently.

The CDH leverages GraphQL via AWS AppSync to build scalable and universal APIs, increasing development flexibility.

Accelerating Innovation

The AWS-based data lake forms the BMW Group’s foundation for developing data-driven IT solutions, enabling faster innovation compared to the previous on-premises solution.

The BMW Group plans to open source key components surrounding the CDH and continue scaling its capabilities for future digital transformation initiatives.
